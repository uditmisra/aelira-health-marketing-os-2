# Competitor: Ironclad

> Last updated: 2026-03-13
> Update this file whenever a significant product, pricing, or narrative change is detected.

## Overview
- **Website:** ironclad.com
- **Positioning:** "The digital contracting platform" — enterprise CLM, positioned as the category leader
- **Primary market:** Enterprise and upper mid-market (500–10,000+ employees); strong in tech, finance, and healthcare
- **Pricing model:** Enterprise pricing, typically $30,000–$150,000+/year depending on users and modules; not self-serve; requires sales call
- **Funding / stage:** ~$333M raised, Series E; last valued at ~$3.2B (2021)

## Their narrative

Ironclad claims to be "the only platform built specifically for legal" — positioning as a comprehensive, workflow-first system that replaces the disconnected tools legal teams cobble together. Their narrative centers on "digital contracts" as a category they're creating, with the implication that paper/email-based contracting is legacy.

Their current marketing leans into AI heavily ("AI-native contract intelligence") after the AI wave. They acquired AI companies and ship AI clause detection, risk scoring, and automated workflows.

Target buyer in their messaging: General Counsel at 500+ employee companies, with a legal ops budget and a legal ops hire already in place.

## Strengths (why they win)

- **Most complete feature set in the mid-to-enterprise segment** — workflows, approvals, redlining, repository, analytics, all deeply built
- **Salesforce integration is best in class** — sales reps can initiate contracts from within SFDC, auto-pulling deal data. For Salesforce-heavy enterprises, this is a strong pull
- **Brand recognition** — they've done the category marketing. "Ironclad" is often the first name a new GC hears when they start looking at CLMs
- **Customer base is sticky** — enterprise contracts, long implementations, high switching costs once embedded
- **Legal-specific workflow engine** — approval chains, conditional routing, reviewer assignment are more sophisticated than most competitors

## Weaknesses (why they lose)

- **Implementation takes months** — common customer complaint is 3–6+ month setup time, requiring a dedicated legal ops resource and often a systems integrator
- **Too complex for small legal teams** — built for teams with a legal ops function. A solo GC or 2-person legal team will be overwhelmed by the configuration options
- **High price point** — minimum viable deal is typically $30K+/year. Cannot win the Series B company spending $8K/year on all software
- **Non-legal stakeholders don't adopt it** — business teams often revert to email/Slack because Ironclad's interface is built for lawyers, not for the salesperson or procurement manager requesting a contract
- **Support and CS are slow at the lower end of their customer base** — mid-market customers report that post-sale support is enterprise-caliber in price but not always in practice
- **AI is bolted on** — their AI features are marketed heavily but customers report mixed quality; perception is that it's a feature catch-up, not a foundational capability
- **[G2 validation — 2026-03-13]:** 3 new G2 reviews in one week, all 4-star, all citing "implementation took longer than expected." Signal is credible because these are satisfied customers (4-star), not angry ones — factual, not venting.

## How to beat them

**When Ironclad is in the deal:**
- Lead with time-to-value: "SpotDraft customers are live in [X] days. What's your Ironclad implementation timeline?"
- Ask the champion: "Do you have a legal ops person dedicated to managing the CLM? Because Ironclad requires one."
- Anchor on the non-legal user: "How do your salespeople request contracts today? How would they do it in Ironclad?"
- Price anchor: "Ironclad's starting price is typically 3–5x what SpotDraft costs at your company size." *(Field-confirmed: $45K/year quote observed in market as of 2026-03-13)*
- Proof: bring a customer reference who switched from Ironclad or compared and chose SpotDraft

**Landmines to plant:**
- "What does the implementation project look like? Who owns it on your side?"
- "What's the vendor's approach to onboarding support — is there a CS person assigned or is it ticketing?"
- "Ask to see the business team's experience — not just the legal team's interface"

**When they invoke "enterprise-grade" credibility (especially in fintech):**
- This objection is board-level risk perception, not a product evaluation. Address the risk frame, not the features.
- "Enterprise-grade" in practice means: Does it have audit trails? Does it have SSO/SOC 2? Does it have customer references in our industry? — SpotDraft can answer all of these.
- Best counter: a peer reference from a comparable-stage fintech who chose SpotDraft. If we don't have one, this is a content/reference gap to close urgently.

**When we lose to Ironclad:**
- Almost always: company is 500+ employees, has a dedicated legal ops person, and values completeness over speed
- Legitimate loss — don't try to win deals where Ironclad is genuinely better fit
