## Email 2 — Education (What Standard Testing Misses)
**Timing:** Day 2
**Template:** Outbound (plain-text style)
**Subject:** Your last lung test probably missed something.
**Preview text:** What spirometry doesn't tell you — and why it matters.

---
Hi {{first_name}},

Most lung tests in Delhi stop at spirometry — a measure of how much air you can move and how fast. It's a useful starting point. It's not the complete picture.

Here's what a basic spirometry doesn't show:

Small airway function. Standard spirometry measures the large airways. Damage in the small airways — where early-stage COPD and pollution-related disease often begins — doesn't show up clearly until it's already significant.

Airway inflammation. The FeNO test measures nitric oxide in your breath — a direct marker of inflammation. This is what distinguishes asthma from COPD when symptoms overlap. Without it, the diagnosis is a guess.

Gas transfer. The DLCO test tells you how well oxygen actually crosses from your lungs into your bloodstream. This is what drops in ILD and post-COVID lung damage — often while spirometry looks near-normal.

At Aelira, one visit covers all four: PFT, FeNO, Lung Oscillometry, and DLCO. 50+ metrics. Your report is ready before you leave.
