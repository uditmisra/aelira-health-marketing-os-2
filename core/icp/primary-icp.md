## Booking / Appointment Signals

What triggers them to seek Aelira out:

1. **Persistent symptom that GP hasn’t explained** — chronic cough lasting 4+ weeks, unexplained breathlessness during routine activities (climbing stairs, walking to the metro). GP has said “it’s nothing serious” but the patient knows something is off.

2. **Delhi pollution spike news cycle** — AQI hits 300+ in October–November, media runs “breathing Delhi air = 25 cigarettes a day” articles. Health-aware urban professional books a lung check "just to see where I stand."

3. **Post-COVID, not feeling 100%** — 3–6 months after COVID recovery, still experiencing fatigue and breathlessness with exertion that wasn’t there before. Searching “post-COVID breathlessness test Delhi.”

4. **Pulmonologist or GP refers for advanced testing** — Doctor wants FeNO to distinguish asthma from COPD, or FOT to assess small airway involvement. Standard spirometry at diagnostic chains is insufficient for this.

5. **COPD/ILD patient referred for rehabilitation** — Pulmonologist tells patient “you need pulmonary rehab” and the patient searches for a dedicated centre vs. hospital department with long waits.

6. **Smoker/ex-smoker self-assessment** — Smoked for 15+ years. Quit or still smoking. Reads about COPD in media, wants to know current lung function. "Better to know than not know."

**What they’ve tried before:**
- **GP consultation only** — got generic advice ("reduce stress, use an inhaler") without clear diagnostic clarity. Felt unsatisfied.
- **General diagnostic centers** (Ganesh Diagnostic, City X-Ray, CNC Path Lab) — got basic spirometry, waited a week for a report with few metrics. Poor experience. Staff indifferent.
- **Hospital OPD** (Max, Fortis, Apollo) — long wait times (2–3 hours), impersonal experience, pulmonology is one department among dozens.
- **"Just living with it"** — most common alternative. Adapted life around breathlessness (took lift instead of stairs, stopped exercising) without seeking diagnosis.

**What made previous solutions fail:**
- Reports with few metrics that didn’t explain what the numbers meant
- Waiting days for results when anxiety was acute
- Being treated as a number in a hospital queue, not a lung patient with a specific concern
- Receiving only spirometry when they needed FOT/FeNO/DLCO for accurate diagnosis
- Rehabilitation programs embedded in hospitals — impersonal, inconvenient to schedule, not lung-specialized

- **Goals:** Understand what’s actually happening to their lungs. Get a clear answer, not medical ambiguity. Have a plan — either "your lungs are fine, here’s what to monitor" or "here’s what you have, here’s what to do."

## Secondary Patient: The COPD/ILD Rehab Candidate

- **Description:** 55–72 year old, diagnosed COPD or ILD patient, referred by pulmonologist. Breathlessness limiting daily activities — can’t climb stairs, can’t carry groceries. Family member often involved in decision and accompaniment.
- **Goals:** Breathe more easily during daily activities. Not become more dependent on family. Reduce fear of exertion. "Adding life to years."
- **Fears:** That rehabilitation won’t work. That it’s too hard for their current condition. That they’ll have to stop halfway.
- **How they measure success:** Walking further without stopping. Doing their daily puja without getting out of breath. Sleeping through the night. Family member noticing improvement.
- **Where they get information:** Pulmonologist’s recommendation is primary. Family member researches on Google/Practo. WhatsApp from other patients.
- **How they decide:** Slow and considered. Family consultation essential. Trust in the centre’s doctor credentials and empathy is the tiebreaker.

## Disqualifiers — Who Is NOT a Good Fit

- **Patients outside Delhi NCR** — Pulmonary rehab requires 3x/week in-person attendance at Green Park. Patients in Lucknow, Mumbai, or even Noida far-east cannot realistically complete the program.
- **Patients seeking acute/emergency care** — Aelira is a diagnostics and rehabilitation centre, not an emergency or acute care facility. Active exacerbations requiring hospitalization must go to a hospital.
- **Patients seeking only a cheap spirometry** — Aelira’s value is in comprehensive testing (50+ metrics, FOT/FeNO/DLCO). Price-only seekers are better served by diagnostic chains at INR 1,500.
- **Insurance-only patients (if Aelira doesn’t take insurance)** — MISSING: need to confirm Aelira’s insurance status. FAQ mentions “Do you accept insurance?” exists but answer not visible in research.
- **Patients needing bronchoscopy, biopsy, or interventional pulmonology** — Aelira is non-invasive diagnostic + rehab only. Invasive procedures must go to hospital.
