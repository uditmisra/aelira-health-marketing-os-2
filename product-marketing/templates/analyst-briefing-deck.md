## Slide 1-2: Company Overview (2 slides, 2 minutes)

**Slide 1 — What we do and for whom**

- Company name, founding year, headquarters
- One-sentence description: what we do and for whom
- Stage and scale signals: ARR range or growth rate, headcount, customer count
- Core use case in one sentence: the job we are hired to do

Tone: factual, not promotional. Analysts have low tolerance for marketing language in slide 1.

**Slide 2 — Market traction**

- 2-3 key logos (recognizable customers in the analyst's coverage area)
- One growth metric (ARR growth %, customer count, or NRR — the most impressive you can share)
- One customer outcome metric (what your customers achieve)
- Any notable third-party validation (press, awards, prior analyst mentions)

---

## Slide 3: Market Context (1 slide, 2 minutes)

**Why this problem matters now**

- The structural market shift that makes this problem urgent today (not "this has always been a problem" — what changed?)
- The cost of the problem at scale
- Why existing approaches are failing (the old way, and why it breaks at the current market moment)
- The gap in the market you are positioned to fill

Tone: category-building perspective, not competitive attack. Analysts respond to clear thinking about markets; they are skeptical of "we are better than X" framings.

---

## Slide 4: Our Approach (1-2 slides, 3 minutes)

**The "new way" — our mechanism and philosophy**

- What makes our approach structurally different from the old way (not just better — different in kind)
- The core product philosophy: what do we believe that our competitors do not?
- 3-4 key capabilities — framed as capabilities (what they enable), not features (what they do)
- Architecture or workflow diagram if it clarifies the approach (optional — only if it actually helps)

Framing: use April Dunford's "positioned against alternatives" lens. What are you specifically better than, and why is your approach more appropriate for the problem?

---

## Slide 5: Customer Evidence (1-2 slides, 3 minutes)

**2-3 named customers with use case and outcome**

For each customer:
- Company name and size (or anonymized descriptor if not approved)
- The specific problem they had
- How they use the product
- The measurable outcome (specific — see case study template for standards)

Selection criteria: choose customers that are recognizable in the analyst's coverage area, representative of the ICP at scale, and able to show before/after.

---

## Slide 6: Roadmap (1 slide, 2 minutes)

**6-month directional roadmap — under NDA**

- 2-3 thematic areas of investment (not feature names — the outcomes those investments unlock)
- Why these bets make sense given the market context
- What this signals about where we believe the market is going

What NOT to include: specific release dates, feature-level detail, anything you're not confident will ship.

Note NDA status clearly on the slide.

---

## Slide 7: Competitive Landscape (1 slide, 2 minutes)

**How we see the market**

- Your view of the competitive landscape: who is in the market, how you segment them, where you sit
- The dimensions that matter most to buyers and where each player stands
- Your honest view of where competitors are strong (analysts respect intellectual honesty)
- Where you have structural advantage

Format: 2x2 matrix with dimensions you define, or a narrative segmentation ("There are three types of solutions in this space..."). Only use a 2x2 if it genuinely clarifies the landscape — not just to put yourself in the top right.

---

## Slide 8: The Ask (1 slide, 1 minute)

**What we want from this analyst**

Be explicit and specific. Options:

- "We're hoping to be included in your upcoming [report name]. What do we need to demonstrate to earn inclusion?"
- "We'd value your perspective on our category narrative — specifically, how you'd position [our category] relative to [adjacent category]."
- "We'd like to establish an inquiry relationship. Who should we contact for future briefings?"
- "We'd like your honest assessment of where you see our differentiation as strong and where you see gaps."

Choose one primary ask. Do not ask for all of them at once.
