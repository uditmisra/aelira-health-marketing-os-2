# Style Guide — SpotDraft

> Last updated: 2026-03-12
> Applies to all external-facing copy: ads, email, web, social, sales materials

## Product naming

- **Company name:** SpotDraft (one word, capital S and D — never "Spot Draft" or "spotdraft")
- **Do not abbreviate:** Do not refer to as "SD" in external copy
- **Product name:** SpotDraft (same as company — no separate product naming tier at this stage)
- **Feature names:** Title Case for named features (e.g., "Playbook Builder," "AI Review," "Contract Repository")
- **Category name:** "contract management" in external copy (lowercase) — avoid "CLM" in B2C-facing material; use it in content aimed at legal ops professionals who know the term

## Capitalization rules

- **Job titles:** Capitalize when used as a formal title before a name ("General Counsel Sarah Chen"), lowercase when used generically ("the general counsel reviewed the contract")
- **Department names:** Lowercase ("the legal team," "in-house legal")
- **Legal terminology:** Follow standard legal usage — "Master Service Agreement," "NDA," "SLA" (acronyms always capitalized)
- **Headers:** Sentence case in body content. Title Case for navigation and product feature names.
- **SpotDraft features:** Always Title Case: Playbook Builder, AI Review, Contract Repository, eSign, Approval Workflows

## Numbers and data

- **Numerals vs. words:** Use numerals for 10 and above (10, 25, 100). Spell out one through nine.
- **Percentages:** Always use numerals + % symbol: "reduces review time by 60%," not "sixty percent"
- **Time:** "3 days" not "three days" when used as a metric or outcome. "6 hours" not "six hours."
- **Currency:** "$1,200/month" — no space between $ and number. Use K for thousands in informal copy: "$120K" not "$120,000"
- **Citing data:** Always cite source in parentheses or footnote for claims used externally: "(SpotDraft customer data, 2025)" or "(Razorpay case study)"
- **Ranges:** Use em dash for ranges: "50–70%" not "50-70%"

## Headers and structure

- **Body copy paragraphs:** Max 3–4 sentences. One idea per paragraph.
- **Lists:** Use bullet points for 3+ parallel items. Full sentences or fragments — but not mixed in the same list.
- **Subheads:** Every section of a long-form piece needs a subhead. Subheads should work as standalone sentences — someone skimming should get the argument from the subheads alone.
- **Bold:** For emphasis only — one or two phrases per page. Not entire sentences.
- **Italics:** For titles of publications, case study names, or genuine emphasis. Not for decoration.

## Email formatting

- Subject lines: Sentence case. Under 50 characters. No clickbait. No "Re:" pretending it's a reply.
- Preview text: Always write it explicitly — don't let the email client pull from body.
- Signature: Plain text preferred. No images in signature.
- CTA button: One per email. Text starts with a verb.

## Social copy

- **LinkedIn:** Professional but not stiff. Lead with a specific observation or data point. Max 3–4 lines before "see more." No hashtag stuffing — max 2 relevant hashtags.
- **Twitter/X:** Not a primary channel. Used for product announcements and content amplification only.

## Legal disclaimers

- Any specific performance claim used externally ("reduces review time by X%") requires customer approval or attribution
- Claims without specific customer attribution should be phrased as "customers report" or "teams using SpotDraft typically"
- Never make claims about competitor products that cannot be verified by a third-party source
