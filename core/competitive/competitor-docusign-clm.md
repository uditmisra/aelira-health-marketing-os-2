# Competitor: DocuSign CLM

> Last updated: 2026-03-13
> Update this file whenever a significant product, pricing, or narrative change is detected.

## Overview
- **Website:** docusign.com/products/clm
- **Positioning:** "CLM from the company you trust" — extends DocuSign's brand halo from e-sign into full contract management
- **Primary market:** Existing DocuSign e-sign customers being upsold to CLM; enterprise and upper mid-market
- **Pricing model:** Module add-on to DocuSign subscription; typically sold alongside existing DocuSign licenses; pricing is opaque, enterprise-grade
- **Funding / stage:** Public company (DOCU); $2.7B revenue (FY2024); CLM is a growth segment but not dominant product

## Their narrative

DocuSign's CLM pitch is trust and consolidation: "You already use DocuSign for signatures — now manage the full contract lifecycle without adding another vendor." The play is reducing vendor sprawl for buyers who are already in the DocuSign ecosystem.

Post-2023 leadership change, DocuSign is pushing "Intelligent Agreement Management (IAM)" as a broader category narrative — moving from "e-sign" to "agreements" as their category. CLM is one module within this platform vision.

## Strengths (why they win)

- **Installed base advantage** — DocuSign has 1M+ customers globally. CLM upsell to existing customers requires no new vendor procurement cycle — just a contract amendment
- **Brand trust** — "DocuSign" is a verb. Brand recognition is unmatched in the e-sign/contract space
- **Single vendor consolidation** — procurement teams and CFOs like fewer vendors; DocuSign CLM wins on simplicity of the buying decision even when the product is inferior
- **E-sign integration is native** — no integration to build; signatures happen in the same ecosystem as the contract workflow
- **Large enterprise sales motion** — strong relationships with procurement at Fortune 500 companies; deals get done through existing relationships, not competitive evaluation

## Weaknesses (why they lose)

- **CLM was acquired, not built** — DocuSign's CLM came from the SpringCM acquisition (2018). The product has improved but the architecture reflects its origin as a separate product bolted onto DocuSign
- **Contract creation is weak** — DocuSign is signature-first. The pre-signature workflow (template creation, redlining, collaboration) is less polished than purpose-built CLM tools
- **AI capabilities are lagging** — competitors and customers report that DocuSign's AI clause review is behind Ironclad and SpotDraft; feature parity without quality parity
- **UX designed for administrators, not users** — configuring DocuSign CLM requires admin expertise; the end-user experience for business teams requesting contracts is clunky
- **Pricing is bundled and opaque** — buyers often don't know what they're getting or paying for until deep in sales; this creates distrust
- **Customer support is enterprise-caliber in price only** — common complaint from mid-market customers is ticket queues and generic support

## How to beat them

**When DocuSign CLM is in the deal (usually: incumbent e-sign customer being upsold):**
- Challenge the consolidation assumption: "DocuSign CLM is an upsell on your e-sign contract. Is buying CLM from your signature vendor the right call, or would you rather have the best CLM?"
- Product demo first: "Let us show you both contract creation and AI review — specifically the stuff that happens before the signature. That's where the time goes."
- Ask about implementation: "How long does DocuSign CLM implementation typically take? Who on your team owns it?"
- Price transparency: SpotDraft pricing is transparent; DocuSign is not — this is a trust signal in evaluation

**When we're trying to displace DocuSign e-sign + CLM:**
- This is a high-friction deal — they own both the e-sign and CLM budget
- Win on product quality for the pre-signature workflow + AI review
- Win on total cost of ownership if they're paying for both DocuSign CLM and still using other tools to fill gaps

**Landmines to plant:**
- "Walk me through how your team creates a new contract from scratch today — in DocuSign CLM."
- "What does the non-legal employee experience look like when they need to request a contract?"
- "How do you track obligations and renewals today? Is that within DocuSign or in another tool?"

## Their customers

- Primarily existing DocuSign e-sign customers in enterprise segments
- Strong in financial services, insurance, real estate (high signature volume industries)
- Not typically the default choice for Series B–D tech companies (they tend to evaluate purpose-built CLMs)
