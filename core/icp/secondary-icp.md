# Secondary ICP — SpotDraft

> Last updated: 2026-03-12
> Lower priority than primary ICP. Worth serving but do not optimize ad spend, content, or product roadmap for this profile at the expense of primary.

## Company profile

- **Industry:** Professional services firms (consulting, accounting, marketing agencies), non-tech companies with high contract volume but no in-house tech-buying culture
- **Size:** 50–500 employees
- **Stage:** Bootstrapped or PE-backed, not VC-backed tech — profitable but growing
- **Geography:** US primary
- **Legal structure:** Often no in-house lawyer — contracts managed by CFO, COO, or office manager
- **Contract volume:** 50–100 contracts/month — enough pain to feel it, less than primary ICP

## Buying signals

- Owner or COO just had a painful contract dispute and wants a paper trail going forward
- Company is scaling a channel partner program and needs to manage partner agreements at volume
- New CFO asks "what contracts do we have and when do they renew?" and no one can answer
- Growing team of account managers or project managers who are manually creating contracts for each client engagement

## Primary buyer

- **Title:** COO, CFO, or Office Manager
- **Goals:** Reduce contract-related admin burden, stop missing renewals, have a clean record of every agreement
- **Fears:** Spending too much on software, buying something the team won't use, getting locked in
- **How they measure success:** "Everything is in one place and I don't have to chase people for contract updates"
- **Price sensitivity:** Higher than primary ICP — will compare to cheaper tools. SpotDraft needs to sell on time-savings ROI clearly.

## Economic buyer

- Same as primary buyer in most cases — COO or CFO writes the check

## End user

- **Title:** Account Manager, Project Manager, or the same COO
- **Day-to-day pain:** Creating contracts from Word templates, emailing PDFs back and forth, using personal DocuSign accounts for signatures, no centralized storage
- **What a great day looks like:** Client requests a contract, they fill in a web form, contract generates automatically, gets signed digitally, files itself in the right folder

## Why this is secondary (not primary)

1. **Longer sales cycle for less ACV** — COO/CFO buyers are more risk-averse and slower to decide than GCs who have a strong mandate to fix the problem
2. **Lower expansion potential** — limited contract volume growth ceiling; primary ICP scales as the company raises and grows
3. **Higher churn risk** — if a startup in this profile doesn't make it past Series A, they cancel; professional services firms are more stable but slower to adopt new tools
4. **Less word-of-mouth leverage** — primary ICP GCs talk to each other at CLOC, ACC events; secondary ICP buyers don't have a community that drives referrals the same way

## How to reach this segment

- Google Ads: "contract management for small business" — high intent, lower CPL than LinkedIn
- SEO: "how to manage contracts without a lawyer" — informational intent, high volume
- Partner channel: accounting software integrations, legal tech marketplaces
- **Do not spend LinkedIn budget** targeting this segment — the targeting taxonomy doesn't fit well and CPL will be too high for the ACV
