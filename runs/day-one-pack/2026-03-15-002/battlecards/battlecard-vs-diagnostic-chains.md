---

## When this card applies
A patient asks:
- "Ganesh Diagnostic is just down the road and much cheaper — why Aelira?"
- "I can get a PFT for ₹1,500 nearby. What's the difference?"
- "I just need a basic lung test, not a full assessment."

---

## The One-Sentence Answer
"A diagnostic lab gives you basic numbers. Aelira gives you a complete picture of your lung health — the kind that tells you what's actually happening and what to do about it."

## Talking Points

**If concern is price:**
> "A basic spirometry for ₹1,500 gives you 6 numbers. If those numbers raise a question — which they often do — you'll need more tests. Aelira's assessment gives you the complete picture in one visit: PFT, FeNO, Oscillometry, and DLCO. The cost of two visits to two different labs often exceeds a single comprehensive assessment at Aelira, and you walk away actually knowing what's happening with your lungs."

**If concern is convenience ("it's closer"):**
> "Convenience is worth considering. But if you're making a trip specifically for a lung test, it's worth making the trip count. Getting 50+ metrics in one session means you have answers — not more follow-up questions."

**If they say "I just need a basic test":**
> "If your doctor wants a routine pre-op spirometry and nothing else is concerning, a basic test might be exactly right. But if you're coming because you're worried about your lungs — because you're getting breathless, or you've been living in Delhi's air for years, or you've had COVID — the basic test often won't tell you what you need to know. We'd suggest a conversation with our team first to figure out which tests make sense for your situation. That conversation is free."

## Key Proof Point to Reference
"Our patients describe their experience as 'the best end-to-end experience I've had.' Diagnostic lab reviews in the same area describe staff who were rude and reports that were inaccurate. The difference isn't just depth of testing — it's the whole experience."

## What NOT to say
- Don't name specific labs negatively in patient-facing materials.
- Don't claim Aelira is cheaper — it isn't. Own the premium positioning.
