# Battlecard: Aelira vs. Hospital Pulmonology Departments
(Max Healthcare, Fortis, Apollo, Yashoda)

**Built by:** competitive-landscape-builder / battlecard agent
**Run ID:** 2026-03-15-002
**For use by:** Patient coordinators, front desk, any team member handling patient enquiries

---

## When this card applies
A patient or family member says:
- "My doctor referred me to Max for a PFT — why should I come to you instead?"
- "Can't I just get this done at Fortis / Apollo?"
- "The hospital is closer. Why Aelira?"
- "Is Aelira as good as a hospital?"

---

## The One-Sentence Answer
"A hospital pulmonology department is built for all conditions. Aelira is built for lungs only — which means more time with you, more comprehensive tests, and your results before you leave."

## Talking Points (pick 2–3 based on patient concern)

**If concern is: "The hospital has better equipment / doctors"**
> "Hospital pulmonologists are excellent. The question is what tests they order for you. At most hospital OPDs, a PFT means a basic spirometry. Aelira adds Lung Oscillometry, which catches small airway disease that spirometry misses, and FeNO, which tells us if airway inflammation is present. These tests are clinically important and rarely available at hospital OPD level in Delhi."

**If concern is: "I trust the hospital brand"**
> "You're right to trust Max / Fortis. For complex interventional procedures, surgeries, or ICU care — they're the right place. For a comprehensive diagnostic assessment of your lung function, or a dedicated rehabilitation programme, a specialist centre like Aelira gives you more depth and a better experience."

**If concern is: "I need to wait for the report anyway"**
> "At most hospital OPDs, a PFT report takes 2–3 days, sometimes longer. At Aelira, you have your full report — 50+ metrics — in your hands before you leave the building. If you're worried, waiting doesn't help. Knowing does."

**If concern is: "My insurance covers the hospital"**
> "That's worth checking — call your insurer to confirm exactly which tests are covered and at which facility. We can help you understand what the tests will cost at Aelira so you can compare. Sometimes out-of-pocket at a specialist centre is close to the hospital co-pay, with a significantly better experience."

---

## What NOT to say
- Don't dismiss hospitals. They are excellent for what they do. The positioning is specialist vs. generalist, not better vs. worse.
- Don't guarantee outcomes or claim superiority without specifics.
- Don't mention competitors by name in written materials — use category language ("hospital OPD") instead.

---

## If the patient still wants to go to the hospital
That's fine. Ask: "Would it help to have a list of which tests to specifically request? Often a referral letter just says 'PFT' — and a technician does basic spirometry. You can request FeNO and DLCO specifically to make sure you get a complete picture."

This positions Aelira as a helpful advisor, not a sales barrier. It also demonstrates expertise. Some of these patients will come back after the hospital experience.
