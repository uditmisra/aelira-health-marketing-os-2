## Their narrative
Max positions itself as a comprehensive, multi-specialty hospital with specialist-grade pulmonology. The message is about scale, breadth, and credentials (“experienced team, cutting-edge technology”). Pulmonology is not a standalone offer — it’s one department within a campus of 30+ specialties. They win on brand trust, insurance tie-ups, and GP referral networks.

## Strengths (why they win)
- Massive brand recognition across Delhi NCR — patients trust “Max” without researching further
- Insurance acceptance — eliminates out-of-pocket cost barrier for many patients
- Strong GP referral pipelines built over 20+ years
- On-campus access to bronchoscopy, HRCT, interventional pulmonology, thoracic surgery (one-stop for complex cases)
- Multiple locations across Delhi

## Weaknesses (why they lose)
- OPD wait times reported as 2–3 hours minimum in pulmonology; patients often see the doctor for 10–15 minutes
- Pulmonology test report turnaround is typically 2–3 days — not same-session
- No standalone pulmonary rehabilitation program as a centre-specific product; rehab is an add-on embedded in physiotherapy
- Impersonal experience — patient is one of 40 OPD patients that day, not a lung patient in a dedicated centre
- No FOT/Oscillometry testing confirmed in research — likely spirometry + DLCO only
- No FeNO testing at most Max locations

## How to beat them
1. **Own the word “dedicated.”** Aelira does one thing: lungs. A hospital does 30 things. For a patient whose lungs are the concern, dedicated beats comprehensive every time.
2. **Lead with speed.** “Results before you leave” vs. Max’s 2–3 day report turnaround is a concrete, meaningful difference for anxious patients.
3. **Advanced testing menu.** FOT/Oscillometry catches small airway disease that spirometry alone misses. Position this as a clinical gap Max doesn’t fill.
4. **Experience, not scale.** Our patients describe the Aelira experience as “the best end-to-end experience I’ve had.” That’s a different promise from Max’s scale-and-credentials story.

## Their customers
All segments of Delhi NCR, weighted toward: insurance-covered patients, GP-referred patients, patients who have heard of Max from family, patients needing complex interventional procedures. Not a specialist population — general population.
