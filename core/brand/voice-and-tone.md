# Brand Voice and Tone — SpotDraft

> Last updated: 2026-03-12
> Every agent that produces copy reads this first.

## Voice (who we are)

SpotDraft sounds like the smartest lawyer in the room who also understands the business. We are:

- **Direct** — we say what we mean. No hedging, no filler, no "in order to."
- **Confident** — we have a strong point of view about how contracting should work. We share it.
- **Peer-to-peer** — we talk to legal professionals as equals. We know their world. We don't need to explain what a redline is.
- **Outcome-obsessed** — we measure everything in results. Not features. Not capabilities. What actually changes for the person doing the work.
- **Dry when appropriate** — legal professionals have a dark, dry sense of humor about their own field. We're allowed to use it sparingly.

## Tone (how we sound)

Baseline: confident and precise. Like a well-written contract — no ambiguity, no fluff, every word doing a job.

Adjust by context:
- **Ads and landing pages:** Tighter, more direct. Problem-first. Every headline a punch.
- **Email:** Slightly warmer. One human talking to another. Short sentences.
- **Long-form content:** More expansive. We can show we understand their world. But still no jargon.
- **Sales enablement:** Clinical and evidence-based. Lawyers respond to specifics, not enthusiasm.

## What we always do

- Lead with the outcome, not the feature ("Close deals 3 days faster" not "Automated clause library")
- Use numbers when we have them. Vague claims earn no trust with lawyers.
- Name the problem before offering the solution. The problem is what they're actually living.
- Write short sentences. Legal professionals read contracts all day. They want relief.
- Respect their expertise — they know what CLM stands for. Don't over-explain the category.

## What we never do

- Use the word "streamline." It means nothing and lawyers know it.
- Use "seamlessly" — nothing is seamless.
- Say "platform" as a benefit — that's a complexity signal.
- Use passive voice in headlines or CTAs.
- Lead with SpotDraft's founding story or team credentials in buyer-facing copy.
- Claim AI does things it doesn't do. Lawyers will test it.
- Use superlatives without proof: "best," "fastest," "most powerful" without data.
- Compare directly to competitors by name in external copy (handle in battlecards instead).

## Words we use

**Product / category:**
- "Contract management" (not "contract lifecycle management" in external copy — too jargony)
- "Contract review" (not "contract analysis")
- "Standard language" or "playbook" (not "clause library" in buyer-facing copy)
- "In-house legal team" (not "legal department" — the former is how they identify)
- "Business team" (not "business stakeholder" — too corporate)
- "Redline" is fine — our audience knows it
- "SpotDraft" — always one word, capital S and D

**Proof language:**
- "X% faster" — specific
- "Teams at [Company] use SpotDraft to..." — social proof with names
- "In [N] hours, not days" — concrete time framing
- "Without [pain they experience]" — name what they escape

## Words we avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Streamline | [specific verb: reduce, cut, eliminate] |
| Seamlessly | [omit or be specific: "in one place"] |
| Robust | [specific capability] |
| Best-in-class | [specific proof point] |
| Platform | SpotDraft, or name the specific feature |
| Digital transformation | [omit] |
| End-to-end | [specific workflow] |
| Leverage | Use |
| Utilize | Use |
| Stakeholders | Legal team / business team / [specific title] |
| Solution | SpotDraft, or the specific capability |
| Empower | [omit — show the outcome instead] |

## Writing style

- **Sentence length:** Short for headlines and CTAs (under 10 words). Medium for body copy (15–20 words max per sentence). Break up anything longer than 25 words.
- **Use of data:** Cite it when you have it. Format: "Customers reduce review time by X%" or "Teams at [Company] cut [metric] from X to Y." Don't hide data in footnotes — put it in the headline.
- **Use of questions:** Use sparingly. When used, they should name the exact pain: "How many contracts are sitting in someone's inbox right now?" Not: "Are you ready to transform your legal operations?"
- **CTAs:** Action verbs only. "See SpotDraft in action" — not "Learn more." "Get your contracts under control" — not "Request a demo." The CTA should name the outcome, not the action.
- **Oxford comma:** Yes, always.
- **Em dash:** Use for emphasis. Not overused. Max one per paragraph.

## Examples of on-brand copy

**On-brand (headline):**
> Your legal team shouldn't be a bottleneck. SpotDraft closes contracts in days, not weeks.

Why it works: Names the pain (bottleneck), frames the outcome (days not weeks), no jargon.

**On-brand (body copy):**
> The average in-house legal team reviews the same standard clauses hundreds of times a year. SpotDraft's playbook library handles routine reviews automatically — so your team spends time on the contracts that actually need a lawyer.

Why it works: Respects the lawyer's expertise, names a specific inefficiency, explains the outcome without overselling.

**On-brand (CTA):**
> See how Razorpay's legal team cut contract turnaround from 5 days to 6 hours.

Why it works: Specific company, specific metric, before/after framing.

## Examples of off-brand copy

**Off-brand:**
> SpotDraft is the robust, end-to-end CLM platform that empowers legal teams to seamlessly streamline their contract lifecycle management processes.

Why it fails: 6 banned words in one sentence. Says nothing. Could apply to any CLM.

**Off-brand:**
> Leverage our AI-powered solution to transform your legal department's digital strategy.

Why it fails: "Leverage," "transform," "solution," "digital strategy" — all meaningless. Reads like a press release from 2018.

**Off-brand:**
> We're passionate about helping legal teams succeed!

Why it fails: Enthusiastic and vague. Lawyers respond to evidence, not passion.
