### Services Section

**Current H2:** "Comprehensive lung care"
**Recommended H2:** "Four tests. One visit. The complete picture."

**Recommended intro copy:**
Most Delhi lung clinics offer spirometry. Aelira offers the full picture: four complementary tests that together tell you things no single test can.

**Service blocks — recommended copy:**

**PFT / Spirometry**
*The foundation.*
Measures airflow, lung volumes, and breathing capacity. The essential starting point for any lung assessment. At Aelira, this is one component of a comprehensive assessment — not the whole story.

**Lung Oscillometry (FOT)**
*What standard tests miss.*
Detects disease in the small airways — where early-stage COPD and pollution-related damage often begins. Uses gentle sound waves, not forced breathing. Suitable for children, elderly patients, and anyone who struggles with traditional spirometry.

**FeNO Test**
*The inflammation answer.*
Measures nitric oxide in your breath — a direct marker of eosinophilic airway inflammation. Distinguishes asthma from COPD when symptoms overlap. Non-invasive. Takes 15 minutes.

**DLCO Test**
*Oxygen transfer, not just airflow.*
Measures how efficiently oxygen crosses from your lungs into your bloodstream — what gets impaired in ILD, pulmonary fibrosis, and post-COVID lung damage. A spirometry result can look near-normal while DLCO has dropped.

### The Problem Section

**Current H2:** "Why pulmonary rehabilitation works"
**Recommended H2:** "Breathlessness creates a cycle. Here's how to break it."

**Recommended body:**
When you feel short of breath with exertion, the natural response is to move less. This feels safe. Physically, it accelerates the problem.

Less movement → muscles decondition → muscles demand more oxygen for less effort → your lungs work harder → you get more breathless.

Pulmonary rehabilitation is specifically designed to reverse this cycle — safely, with clinical supervision, starting at whatever level you can manage.

## Diagnostic Test Pages — Recommended Copy (one example: FOT page)

### FOT / Lung Oscillometry Page

**H1:** "Lung Oscillometry (FOT): The Lung Test That Doesn't Ask You to Force Anything"

**Subheadline:** "Detects small airway disease that standard spirometry misses. Works for children, elderly patients, and anyone who finds forced breathing difficult."

**What is it?**
Lung Oscillometry — also called the FOT (Forced Oscillation Technique) or impulse oscillometry — measures airway resistance by sending gentle sound-wave pulses through your lungs while you breathe normally.

You don't blow as hard as you can. You breathe at your normal rate. The machine does the analysis.

**Why it matters in Delhi:**
PM2.5 particles — the finest, most harmful pollutants in Delhi's air — penetrate into the small airways first. Small airway disease often develops years before it produces noticeable symptoms or shows up on standard spirometry. FOT is the test that catches it early.

**Who needs it:**
- Anyone with mild or early-stage breathlessness where spirometry came back "normal"
- Children with recurring cough or wheeze (no forced breathing required)
- Elderly patients or post-surgery patients who struggle with standard spirometry
- Anyone whose doctor suspects COPD but symptoms and spirometry don't fully align
- Long-term Delhi residents who want to assess pollution-related small airway impact
